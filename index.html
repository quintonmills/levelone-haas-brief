Solutions Architecture Case Study

LevelOne

A Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS) ecosystem for industrial liquid tracking and predictive infrastructure analytics.

The Problem Space

Traditional liquid asset management relies on manual inspections, leading to high operational costs and "blind spots" in critical infrastructure. LevelOne solves this by bridging physical sensor data with a real-time digital intelligence layer.

System Architecture

Built on a distributed Edge-to-Cloud model. The architecture prioritizes data integrity and power efficiency for remote deployments.

Architectural Trade-offs

PostgreSQL vs. NoSQL

Chose relational integrity to manage the complex hierarchy between municipal nodes and physical sensor assets, ensuring auditability for public infrastructure.

REST vs. WebSockets

Selected RESTful ingestion to minimize battery drain on edge devices, utilizing a sleep-wake protocol that prioritizes hardware lifespan over millisecond-level streaming.

Business Impact

LevelOne transforms a "reactive" maintenance model into a "proactive" strategic asset. This architectural approach allows municipal leaders to anticipate depletion events and optimize logistics, mirroring the scale of enterprise systems like FuelNet.
